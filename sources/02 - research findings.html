Research Findings:
Participant profiles:

The 24 research participants were between the ages of 18-45.

The demographics of the 20 participants who were told to complete a survey are as follow:
Young Adult (ages 18 - 25): 8 participants
Mid-age users (ages 26 - 35): 9 participants

Older Users (ages 36 - 45 : 3 participants

The demographics of the four interview participants are as follows:

Age Range: 19 - 25 years of age

Gender Distribution: 2 male, 2 female

School: Business, Media Arts, UI/UX, Video Production

Identified Needs:

Data efficiency:
        Some participants mentioned that an audio-only mode would allow them to save a lot of data. This feature would be particularly good for users with poor internet access or data limits. An audio-only mode would allow for faster load times to prevent buffering and interruptions from occurring.

Multitasking:

Users expressed that an audio-only mode would allow them to interact with the app while performing other tasks. Some users mentioned how they would use an audio-only feature to listen to podcasts or music while commuting. Another user mentioned how it would allow them to listen to audio content while playing video games as an audio-only mode would not affect the internet speed while gaming.

Awareness of Third-Party Services:

        Users are aware of third-party services that would allow for an audio-only playback but they are often unreliable. 40% of users in the study were aware of third-party services that would allow for them to listen to YouTube in an audio-only format. This shows that there is an unmet need for users to have a reliable way to have an audio-only feature within YouTube.

Identified Pain Points/Challenges:

Internet Speed:

        Users with slower internet speeds encounter more buffering issues when watching videos. 40% of participants reported that they experience frequent buffering while watching/listening to YouTube due to poor internet connection.

Battery Drain:

Watching videos on a phone not only takes up more data but also drains the battery life. Users who are keeping the video active while primarily using the app for audio purposes significantly drains the battery. An audio-only mode would allow users to use the app for longer during activities that take a significant time to complete such as commuting and studying.

Unnecessary Video Content:

        While YouTube is primarily a video-streaming platform, many creators create content that is primarily used for audio such as podcasts and music. While these podcasts and songs may have videos associated with them, the main content of the YouTube video is the audio content. Users who consume these types of YouTube videos without actually watching the video will drain their device batteries quicker and they may also encounter buffering issues.

Key Themes:

Strong Interest in an audio only feature:

A significant number of participants expressed a strong interest in an audio-only feature. 8 of participants selected ‘Definitely’ as their level of interest for the feature to be implemented. 8 other participants selected ‘Maybe’ and ‘Neutral’ as their options. Over half of the participants would be interested in an audio-only feature being added to YouTube.

Data and Battery Efficiency:

Research participants were conscious of how much data and battery life is being consumed when using the YouTube app. Participants were aware that watching YouTube videos while primarily consuming it for audio content. Many participants expressed that an audio-only setting would allow for lower data usages, less buffering/interruptions, and less battery drain.

Users using YouTube Multitasking:

        Participants expressed how an audio-only feature would allow them to use YouTube while performing other tasks. Some participants expressed how their slow internet speed does not allow them to play online video games while listening to a YouTube video without encountering interruptions. Another participant expressed how an audio-only feature would be a great way to pass the time while commuting. If an audio-only setting was implemented to YouTube more users would use the app while multitasking.
