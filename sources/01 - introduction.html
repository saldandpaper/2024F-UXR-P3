Introduction

The purpose of this research was to evaluate the user demand for an audio-only feature in the YouTube mobile app. Having an audio-only feature would address user needs such as saving data, enabling multitasking, and improving app accessibility.

The research study was conducted over a nine day period with 24 participants aged between 18 to 45. The participants were recruited through Discord, social media, and direct communication. 20 of the participants were told to complete a 10-question survey. This survey included questions about the users frequency on YouTube, internet speeds, and interest in YouTube implementing an audio-only feature. The four remaining participants were interviewed in-person to gather more quantitative findings. The interviews asked participants about their YouTube usage habits, situations participants found themselves using YouTube only for audio, and how beneficial an audio-only feature would be for the research participant.

This research study uses both survey findings and in-depth interviews to determine if there is enough user interest to justify implementing an audio-only feature to YouTube.
