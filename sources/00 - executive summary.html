Executive Summary

Background:

  • A research study was conducted with 24 participants to determine if YouTube should implement an audio-only feature.
  • 20 participants were given a survey with 10 questions, these questions asked participants about their YouTube usage habits, issues with buffering, and overall interest in an audio-only feature
  • A series of eight questions were created to interview the remaining four participants. These questions were made to gather in-depth insights from users.

Key Findings:

  • 65% of participants use YouTube frequently. 45% of the participants reported that they use YouTube on a daily occurrence and 20% of participants use the app several times a week.
  • The survey asked participants for their interest in an audio-only feature in YouTube.  40% of participants expressed that they would definitely use an audio-only feature on YouTube if they had access to it.
  • Users are concerned about data usage and battery life when using YouTube only for audio purposes.
  • 40% of participants are aware of third-party apps that allow for an audio-only Youtube experience. This shows an unmet need that YouTube could provide.
  • Users frequently mentioned how they would use an audio-only feature to use YouTube while performing other tasks such as commuting, exercising, and studying.

Recommendations:

  • Implement an audio-only setting to YouTube videos.
  • Redesign background playback experience to optimize usability for an audio-only setting.
  • Implement onboarding experience for the new audio-only setting.
  • Continue to grab feedback post-launch of the audio-only feature.
