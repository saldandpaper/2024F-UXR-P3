Executive Summary

Background:

  • A research study was conducted with 24 participants to determine if YouTube should implement an audio-only feature.
  • 20 participants were given a survey with 10 questions, focusing on YouTube usage habits, buffering issues, and interest in an audio-only feature.
  • Four participants were interviewed with eight questions to gather in-depth insights.

Key Findings:

  • 65% of participants use YouTube frequently. Of these, 45% use it daily, and 20% use it several times a week.
  • 40% of participants expressed interest in using an audio-only feature on YouTube.
  • Participants raised concerns about data usage and battery life when using YouTube for audio purposes.
  • 40% of participants are aware of third-party apps offering audio-only YouTube experiences, indicating an unmet need.
  • Participants frequently mentioned they would use an audio-only feature while commuting, exercising, or studying.

Recommendations:

  • Implement an audio-only setting for YouTube videos.
  • Redesign the background playback experience to optimize usability for audio-only usage.
  • Develop an onboarding experience for the new audio-only setting.
  • Continue gathering feedback post-launch of the audio-only feature.
