Data Analysis

Data Collection Methods

  • Surveys: Shared on platforms like Discord, Facebook, Instagram, and personal networks. A total of 20 participants completed the survey, providing quantitative data and qualitative insights through open-ended questions.
  • Interviews: Conducted with three participants to gather detailed feedback on YouTube habits, challenges with video playback, and opinions on an audio-only feature.

Data Analysis Techniques

Quantitative Analysis

  • Frequency Distribution: Analyzed survey responses to identify patterns in YouTube usage and playback challenges.
  • Data Visualization: Used pie charts and bar graphs to represent trends like daily usage and interest in audio-only features.
  • Cross-Tabulation: Compared demographic data (e.g., age) with responses to understand perceptions of the audio-only feature.

Qualitative Analysis

  • Thematic Analysis: Reviewed open-ended responses and interview transcripts to identify recurring themes such as data-saving needs and multitasking preferences.
  • Keyword Frequency: Highlighted terms like "data," "audio," and "playback" to prioritize user needs.
  • Synthesis of Insights: Combined findings from both analyses to create a comprehensive picture of user needs and challenges.

Key Findings

Quantitative Data

  • YouTube Usage Frequency: 45% use YouTube daily, 20% several times a week.
  • Interest in Audio-Only Feature: 40% would "definitely" use it, 20% are "maybe" interested.
  • Internet Speed Issues: 40% experience buffering, while 30% report excellent connectivity.
  • Awareness of Third-Party Tools: 40% are aware of audio-only tools, though many find them unreliable.

Qualitative Data

  • Pain Points: High data usage and battery drain during video playback were common frustrations.
  • User Scenarios: Participants frequently cited multitasking needs (e.g., commuting, working) as reasons for preferring audio-only options.
  • Feedback: Positive feedback included comments like "An audio-only mode would be a game-changer." Some participants preferred video and expressed less interest.

Actionable Insights

  • Introduce an audio-only toggle to address data consumption and battery life concerns.
  • Prioritize features that enhance multitasking capabilities, such as seamless playback during other activities.

Limitations

  • Sample size of 20 participants may limit generalizability.
  • Participants were primarily tech-savvy, potentially skewing results toward users familiar with YouTube alternatives.

By combining quantitative trends with qualitative feedback, the research highlights a clear demand for an audio-only feature, addressing critical user needs and enhancing the overall experience.
